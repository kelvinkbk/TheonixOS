Standing on the shoulders of giants

Theonix OS is a small project with a big debt of gratitude. It’s assembled and maintained by one developer, but it only exists because of the enormous free and open-source ecosystem it’s built on. Here’s an honest look at who and what makes it possible.

Built on the shoulders of giants

The upstream projects we rely on

Theonix is a thin layer of intent over a mountain of open-source work. Enormous thanks to the maintainers and communities behind each of these.

Arch Linux

The rolling-release base and Pacman package manager that Theonix is built directly on.

KDE / Plasma

The Plasma 6 desktop environment and application suite that gives Theonix its look and feel.

Calamares

The distribution-independent installer framework powering Theonix’s guided setup.

Wayland

The modern display protocol Theonix runs on by default for a smooth, secure desktop session.

systemd

The init system and service manager that boots Theonix and keeps its services running.

GRUB

The GNU bootloader that gets the system started on both UEFI and BIOS machines.

Pacman

Arch’s fast, dependable package manager, used to install and update Theonix and its repository.

Flatpak

Sandboxed application distribution, offered alongside Pacman for extra software choice.

freedesktop.org

The shared standards and specifications that let the Linux desktop stack work together.

Linux kernel

The kernel at the heart of it all — the foundation every other piece is built upon.

Names and trademarks belong to their respective projects. Theonix OS is an independent distribution and is not affiliated with or endorsed by these projects.
