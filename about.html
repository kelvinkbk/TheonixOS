About

The story of Theonix

Theonix OS is an independent, community-facing Linux distribution built on Arch. It exists to make a modern, rolling Arch system feel coherent and ready to use — without hiding the power underneath.

Mission

To assemble a cohesive desktop on top of Arch Linux — a branded installer, a curated repository and a themed KDE Plasma 6 experience — so that a rolling-release system is approachable from the first boot while staying fully in the user’s control.

Vision

A Linux desktop that is modern, honest and open — fast to install, pleasant to live in, and transparent about what it is. No lock-in, no dark patterns, no invented promises: just a well-put-together Arch derivative that keeps improving in the open.

Goals

Coherent by default

Installer, repository, theme and branding that fit together.

Fast & modern

KDE Plasma 6 on Wayland with a lean, quick boot path.

Always current

A rolling release — install once, keep updating.

In the open

Developed publicly and released under the GPL.

Creator & maintainer

Theonix OS is built and maintained by Kelvin Benny Koshy as an independent project — from the Arch base and the custom Calamares installer to the package repository, branding and desktop defaults. It’s a solo-led effort that welcomes contributors.

GitHub Community

Open-source philosophy

Theonix stands on the shoulders of the free-software community — Arch Linux, KDE, Calamares, Wayland, systemd and countless upstream projects. In return it is developed transparently and released under the GPL. Bugs, ideas and packaging help are all welcome.

  • ✓ Free and open source, forever
  • ✓ Built on and giving back to upstream
  • ✓ No telemetry, no lock-in
